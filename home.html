PROBLEM: For generations, women in Northern Ghana have been the primary producers of shea butter, an indigenous resource highly prized in the global cosmetics and food industry. There are approximately 600,000 to 800,000 shea butter producers in Ghana, each with extended family and children. Over 80% of these shea butter producers in Ghana are reported to live below the poverty line, despite their critical role in feeding the global cosmetic needs.
Overview: local women in Ghana sell 1kg of shea butter for under $2, whereas 1kg of the same butter is sold in the international market for as much as $300. Thus, these local women receive only 0.6% of the global shea butter market share - despite doing over 90% of the processing work needed.
